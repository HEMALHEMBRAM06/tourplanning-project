About the Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, nestled on the banks of the Yamuna River in Agra, India, is a white marble mausoleum commissioned by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in 1632. It was built in loving memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. This architectural masterpiece combines Persian, Islamic, and Indian styles and stands as a symbol of eternal love. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Taj Mahal is celebrated for its symmetry, exquisite domes, and intricate carvings. It is one of the New Seven Wonders of the World and attracts millions of visitors each year.
